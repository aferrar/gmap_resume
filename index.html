HUNTER COLLEGE - CITY UNIVERSITY OF NEW YORK

I was awarded my Masters degree in Geoinformatics in May of 2018 from the Geography Department at Hunter College.

As a graduate student I was exposed to a number of geographic concepts and theories. I also worked with technologies like ArcGIS Online, CartoDB, Tableau, and RStudio.

My thesis work focused on the integration of version control systems within Open Government Data platforms to promote Citizen Science innovations.

PENNSYLVANIA STATE UNIVERSITY

In 2016 I completed an online graduate certificate program with the Department of Renewable Energy and Sustainability Systems at Penn State University.

During this period I learned an immense amount regarding environmental public policy, stakeholder assessments, renewable energy generation methods, and conducted a number of LCA and SWOT analyses.

If you'd like you can read some of my reports from this program under the Projects tab to the left.

THE CITY COLLEGE OF NEW YORK

I graduated from CCNY in May of 2014 with a degree in Geology from the Department of Earth and Atmospheric Sciences.

During this time I enrolled and excelled in courses directly associated with understanding complex Earth systems, such as Sedimentology, Hydrology, and Atmospheric Science. I conducted extensive fieldwork collecting GPS coordinates and groundwater samples and succinctly communicated my results in the form of academic reports.

In addition, I gained valuable laboratory experience as a research assistant investigating controlled interactions of bacteria, bacteriophage, and inorganic clays to replicate and model natural dynamics.

THE HIGH SCHOOL FOR MATH, SCIENCE, & ENGINEERING

I graduated from HSMSE in 2008.

While there I had the opportunity to take courses related to digital electronics, architectural design, and advanced mathematics for college credit.

DEPARTMENT OF TRANSPORTATION - ASSET MANAGEMENT DIVISION

I am working with DOT in the Asset Management division. I have been tasked with maintaining various geodatabases with python scripts to automate regularly modifying tasks in large datasets.

I have contributed to the development of a citywide predictive Street Deterioration Model using R, SQL, and Python.

I have managed the analysis of historical commitments for all DOT bridge, street, and facility assetsseeking to spatialize and evaluate funding patterns within various political boundaries.

Additionally, I have led the collection and digitization of all NYC street ownership records. Worked in collaboration with Department of City Planning and the respective City Borough President's Topography Departments. Developed QA process for evaluating digitization metrics.
